Did you know?!

  1. It takes 400 cocoa beans to make one pound of chocolate.
  2. Chocolate comes from fruit tree; it's made from a seed.
  3. Each cacao tree produces approximately 2,500 beans.
  4. The average serving of milk chocolate has about the same amount of caffeine as a cup of decaf coffee.
  5. Because cacao trees are so delicate, farmers lose, on average, 30 percent of their crop each year.
  6. “Cacao” is how you say “cocoa” in Spanish.
  7. It is poisonous for dogs and cats
  8. A chocolate river existed for a short time back in 1971 for the movie Willy Wonka & The Chocolate Factory. I was made of 15,000 gallons of water, chocolate, and cream and spoiled failry quickly, leaving a terrible smell.
  9. In Mayan times, the cocoa bean was used as currency.
  10. White chocolate isn't considered real chocolate because it's made using cocoa butter instead of cocoa solids or cocoa liquor.
  11. The chocolate chip cookie was an accident discovered by Ruth Wakefield in 1930. She ran out of baker's chocolate, so tried to put in broken pieces of Nestle chocolate expecting chocolate cookies. Instead, it created the famouse chocolate chip cookies that was then sold to Nestle for a lifetime supply of chocolate.
